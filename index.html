About Me

I was born and raised in Mumbai, India. As a child, there was something about the sky that always intrigued me and got me curious. I would often look up for hours wondering what was beyond all of the darkness that covered us. My childhood dream was to become a Pilot, then I wanted to become an Astronaut, and then I wanted to build rockets. So opting for Engineering was never something that I second-guessed. After I cleared JEE-Advanced (one of the toughest engineering entrance exams in India, with a success rate of 0.5%), my parents moved to Manama City, Bahrain and I started my undergraduate studies at IITK.
My aim is to build a working prototype of propellant storage device that is able to refuel satellites in orbit. I not only want to design it, but also build it and hence my background of Material Science along with Aerospace Engineering. To store cryogenic propellant in space is a huge challenge and hence I am currently working on projects that revolve around the thermal control systems of spacecraft. As I keep learning, I wish to explore the fields of Propulsion, and Machine Learning as tool for mission design and planning. At the end of the day, I just want to be around people who are working hard to send humans to the Moon and beyond.
